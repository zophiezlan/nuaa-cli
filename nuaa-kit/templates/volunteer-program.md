## 1. Program Overview

### Purpose

**Why Volunteers?**

Volunteers bring passion, lived experience, diverse skills, and community connection to NUAA's work. This framework ensures volunteers have meaningful, supported, and rewarding experiences while contributing to harm reduction.

**Program Objectives**:

1. [Objective 1 - e.g., Provide meaningful pathways for community participation]
2. [Objective 2 - e.g., Build capacity and skills in volunteers]
3. [Objective 3 - e.g., Enhance program delivery with volunteer support]
4. [Objective 4 - e.g., Create pathways to peer work employment]
5. [Objective 5 - e.g., Strengthen peer networks and community]

---

### Volunteer Principles

**NUAA's Volunteer Principles**:

- **Meaningful**: Volunteers do work that matters
- **Supported**: Training, supervision, resources provided
- **Safe**: Physical, emotional, and cultural safety prioritized
- **Flexible**: Accommodating varying capacities and circumstances
- **Valued**: Recognition and appreciation
- **Pathway**: Volunteering as step toward peer work employment
- **Reciprocal**: Volunteers benefit as much as NUAA

## 2. Volunteer Roles

### Role 1: Peer Support Volunteer

**Role Description**:

Support people who use drugs through outreach, peer forums, and one-on-one connection.

**Activities**:

- Attend peer forums and community events
- Support outreach activities (distribute resources, connect with community)
- One-on-one peer support (listening, information sharing, referrals)
- Share lived experience (where comfortable)

**Time Commitment**: [2-4 hours per week or 1 day per week]

**Skills Needed**:

- Lived experience of drug use
- Non-judgmental, empathetic approach
- Good communication skills
- Reliability

**Training Provided**:

- Orientation to NUAA
- Harm reduction principles
- Peer support skills
- Boundaries and self-care
- Overdose response and naloxone

### Application Process

**Step 1: Expression of Interest**

- Online form, email, or in-person conversation
- Basic details: name, contact, interests, availability

**Step 2: Informal Interview/Conversation**

- Meet with Volunteer Coordinator
- Discuss interests, motivations, needs
- Explain volunteer roles and expectations
- Assess fit (mutual - does this work for both NUAA and volunteer?)

**Step 3: Background Check** (if required by role/funder)

- Working with Children Check (if working with minors)
- Police Check (only if required - not assumed)
- **Note**: A record should not automatically disqualify - assess individual circumstances with harm reduction lens

**Step 4: Onboarding**

- Welcome and orientation
- Training
- Introduce to team
- First volunteer shift supported

**Timeline**: Aim to onboard within 2-4 weeks of initial contact

## 4. Volunteer Onboarding & Training

**Content**:

1. **Welcome & Introductions** (30 min)

   - Icebreaker activity
   - Volunteer stories (why they're here)

   - Current programs and services

3. **Volunteer Role & Expectations** (30 min)

4. **Health, Safety & Wellbeing** (30 min)

   - Support available

5. **Systems & Practicalities** (30 min)
   - Office tour
   - Timesheets and record-keeping
   - Equipment and resources
   - Who to ask for help

6. **Questions & Next Steps** (15 min)

## 5. Volunteer Policies & Expectations

### Volunteer Rights

**Volunteers have the right to**:

- Be treated with respect and dignity
- Work in a safe environment
- Clear role descriptions and expectations
- Training and support
- Recognition and appreciation
- Feedback on their contribution
- Decline tasks outside their role
- Raise concerns and have them addressed
- Leave the volunteer program at any time

---

### Volunteer Responsibilities

**Volunteers are expected to**:

- Uphold NUAA values and harm reduction principles
- Attend agreed shifts or give notice if unable
- Respect confidentiality and privacy
- Work cooperatively with staff and other volunteers
- Ask for help when needed
- Provide feedback and suggestions
- Complete timesheets and records
- Participate in supervision (as appropriate to role)

---

### Code of Conduct

**Volunteers must**:

- **Respect**: Treat all people with dignity and respect
- **Non-discrimination**: No discrimination based on drug use, gender, sexuality, race, religion, disability, etc.
- **Confidentiality**: Maintain strict confidentiality
- **Boundaries**: Maintain professional boundaries
- **Safety**: Follow safety protocols
- **Honesty**: Be honest and accountable
- **Sobriety during shifts**: Not intoxicated while volunteering (though current drug use outside volunteering is not a barrier)

**Unacceptable Behavior**:

- Violence or threats
- Harassment or bullying
- Discrimination
- Breach of confidentiality
- Theft
- Intoxication while volunteering

**Consequences**: Issue will be addressed through conversation and support; serious or repeated breaches may result in ending volunteer placement

---

### Privacy & Confidentiality

- Volunteers sign confidentiality agreement
- Only access information needed for their role
- No sharing of participant information outside NUAA
- No photos/videos without consent
- Mandatory reporting obligations explained

---

### Insurance

- Volunteers covered by NUAA's public liability insurance while volunteering
- Volunteers cover own personal belongings
- Incidents must be reported immediately
