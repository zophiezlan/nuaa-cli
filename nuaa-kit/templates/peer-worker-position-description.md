## About NUAA

NSW Users & AIDS Association (NUAA) is a peer-led organization with over 30 years of experience working alongside people who use drugs. We are the state-wide peak body representing the interests and rights of people who use illicit drugs in NSW. NUAA operates on harm reduction principles, prioritizing the health, dignity, and rights of our community.

**Our Values**:

- **Peer-led**: People with lived experience at the center of everything we do
- **Harm reduction**: Non-judgmental, evidence-based, meeting people where they're at
- **Advocacy**: Challenging stigma, discrimination, and unjust policies
- **Community**: Building connection, support, and collective voice
- **Respect**: Honoring dignity, diversity, and rights of all people

## Key Responsibilities

### Direct Peer Support & Engagement (60% of role)

- **Outreach**: Engage with people who use drugs in community settings
- **One-on-one support**: Provide information, listening, practical assistance
- **Group facilitation**: Co-facilitate peer support sessions, workshops, activities
- **Resource provision**: Distribute harm reduction supplies, information, resources
- **Referrals**: Connect people to health, social, and support services
- **Follow-up**: Maintain contact with participants, check-in on wellbeing
