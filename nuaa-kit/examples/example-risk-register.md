## Purpose of Risk Register

This register identifies, assesses, and tracks risks that could impact program success. It ensures proactive risk management and supports continuous monitoring.

**Risk Management Principles**:

- **Proactive**: Identify risks early
- **Transparent**: Open communication about risks
- **Participatory**: Team and stakeholder input
- **Adaptive**: Regular review and updates
- **Safety-focused**: Participant and staff wellbeing prioritized

---

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Likelihood Scale

| Rating      | Description                           | Probability |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| 1 - Rare    | May occur only in exceptional circumstances | < 10%   |
| 2 - Unlikely| Could occur at some time              | 10-30%      |
| 3 - Possible| Might occur at some time              | 30-50%      |
| 4 - Likely  | Will probably occur in most circumstances | 50-80%  |
| 5 - Almost Certain | Expected to occur              | > 80%       |

---

### Impact/Consequence Scale

| Rating         | Description                                          | Impact            |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| 1 - Insignificant | Minor inconvenience, easily resolved             | Minimal           |
| 2 - Minor      | Some disruption, manageable with effort              | Low               |
| 3 - Moderate   | Moderate disruption, requires resources to address   | Medium            |
| 4 - Major      | Significant disruption, serious consequences         | High              |
| 5 - Severe     | Critical disruption, catastrophic consequences       | Very High         |

---

### Risk Rating Matrix

**Risk Level = Likelihood × Impact**

| Likelihood | Impact: 1 | Impact: 2 | Impact: 3 | Impact: 4 | Impact: 5 |
| ---------- | --------- | --------- | --------- | --------- | --------- |
| 5          | 5 - Med   | 10 - High | 15 - High | 20 - Ext  | 25 - Ext  |
| 4          | 4 - Low   | 8 - Med   | 12 - High | 16 - Ext  | 20 - Ext  |
| 3          | 3 - Low   | 6 - Med   | 9 - Med   | 12 - High | 15 - High |
| 2          | 2 - Low   | 4 - Low   | 6 - Med   | 8 - Med   | 10 - High |
| 1          | 1 - Low   | 2 - Low   | 3 - Low   | 4 - Low   | 5 - Med   |

**Risk Levels**:
- **1-4**: Low (Monitor)
- **5-9**: Medium (Manage actively)
- **10-15**: High (Priority action required)
- **16-25**: Extreme (Immediate action, senior management involvement)

## Risk Review Process

**Frequency**:
- **Monthly**: Risk register reviewed by Program Coordinator
- **Quarterly**: Risk register reviewed by management team
- **Ad-hoc**: When new risks identified or significant changes occur
- **Annual**: Comprehensive risk register review and update

**Review Checklist**:
- ☐ Are all risks still relevant?
- ☐ Have any risks materialized?
- ☐ Are new risks emerging?
- ☐ Are mitigation strategies working?
- ☐ Do risk ratings need updating?
- ☐ Are responsible parties still appropriate?

---

## Incident Reporting

**If a risk materializes**:

1. **Immediate Response**: Address immediate safety/wellbeing
2. **Notify**: Alert responsible person and management
3. **Document**: Complete incident report
4. **Investigate**: Review what happened and why
5. **Learn**: Update risk register and mitigation strategies
6. **Follow-Up**: Support affected parties, implement changes
