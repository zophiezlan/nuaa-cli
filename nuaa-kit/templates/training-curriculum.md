## 2. Training Principles

### Pedagogical Approach

- **Experiential**: Learning by doing, reflection on experience
- **Participatory**: Active engagement, not lecture-based
- **Peer-led**: Facilitated by people with lived experience
- **Strengths-based**: Building on existing knowledge and skills
- **Culturally safe**: Respectful of diversity, trauma-informed
- **Harm reduction**: Non-judgmental, evidence-based, meeting people where they're at

---

### Training Values

- **Respect**: For all participants' experiences and perspectives
- **Confidentiality**: What's shared in training stays in training
- **Safety**: Emotional and physical safety prioritized
- **Inclusion**: Accessible to people of all backgrounds and abilities
- **Empowerment**: Building confidence and capacity

### Facilitation Tips

**Creating Safe Space**:

- Clear confidentiality agreement
- Ground rules co-created with group
- Respect for diverse experiences
- Trauma-informed approach (content warnings, breaks, opt-out options)

**Engaging Participants**:

- Mix of presentation, discussion, activities
- Small group and large group formats
- Opportunities for individual reflection
- Movement/energizers between sessions

**Managing Challenging Situations**:

- Dominating participant: Thank for contributions, invite others
- Conflict: Refer to ground rules, mediate respectfully
- Disclosure of trauma: Acknowledge, support, offer private conversation
- Disengagement: Check-in during break, adjust activities

**Cultural Safety**:

- Use inclusive language
- Acknowledge Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander peoples
- Respect for LGBTIQ+ identities
- Accessibility considerations
- Multiple ways to participate (verbal, written, creative)
