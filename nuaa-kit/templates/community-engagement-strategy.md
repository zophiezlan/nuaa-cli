- **Transparency**: Open communication about decisions and processes
- **Safety**: Physical, emotional, and cultural safety prioritized
- **Long-term**: Building sustained relationships, not one-off consultations

## 3. Engagement Approaches

### Spectrum of Engagement

| Level       | Definition                            | When to Use                    | Examples                          |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| **Inform**  | Provide information                   | Updates, awareness raising     | Newsletter, social media, flyers  |
| **Consult** | Seek input on specific questions      | Program design, evaluation     | Surveys, focus groups             |
| **Involve** | Ongoing participation in decisions    | Advisory groups, co-design     | Consumer advisory committee       |
| **Collaborate** | Partnership in decision-making    | Joint program delivery         | Peer-led sessions                 |
| **Empower** | Community leads and controls          | Peer governance, advocacy      | Peer workers, community campaigns |

**Our Approach**: Aim for **Collaborate** and **Empower** wherever possible, with **Inform** and **Consult** as minimum.

## 6. Reducing Barriers to Engagement

### Practical Barriers

**Transport**:
- Reimbursement for public transport or parking
- Meet in accessible locations (near public transport)
- Outreach (go to where people are)

**Timing**:
- Flexible scheduling (various times/days)
- Respect that people have chaotic lives
- Drop-in options where possible

**Caring Responsibilities**:
- Childcare provided or funding
- Family-friendly events

**Cost**:
- All engagement free to community
- Remuneration for time and expertise

---

### Social/Emotional Barriers

**Stigma & Shame**:
- Non-judgmental, welcoming environment
- Peer-led spaces (reduce power imbalance)
- Confidentiality protected
- Normalize drug use (not requiring disclosure if uncomfortable)

**Trauma**:
- Trauma-informed approach
- Safe spaces and boundaries
- Choice in level of engagement
- Support available

**Distrust**:
- Transparency about purpose and use of input
- Demonstrate how community input shapes decisions
- Consistent presence and follow-through
- No tokenism

---

### Systemic Barriers

**Exclusion from Services**:
- Open access (no prerequisites)
- No discrimination based on drug use, housing status, etc.

**Language & Literacy**:
- Plain language
- Visual/creative methods
- Verbal options (not just written)

**Cultural Safety**:
- Culturally appropriate methods and spaces
- Cultural advisors and peer workers
- Respect for cultural protocols

## 8. Ethical Community Engagement

### Informed Consent

- Clear explanation of purpose, how input will be used
- Voluntary participation (right to say no, withdraw)
- Consent for specific uses (e.g., quotes, photos)

### Confidentiality

- What's shared in community spaces stays confidential (unless risk of harm)
- Data de-identified
- Participant stories only shared with explicit consent

### Do No Harm

- Risk assessment for engagement activities
- Trauma-informed approach
- Support and referrals available
- Safety (naloxone on-site, first aid)

### Power Sharing

- Community has real decision-making power (not just token consultation)
- Transparent about what's negotiable and what's not
- Feedback loops (community sees impact of their input)

### Respect & Dignity

- No stigmatizing language or imagery
- Value all contributions
- Cultural safety and inclusivity
- Challenge discrimination
