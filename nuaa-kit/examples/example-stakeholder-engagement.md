## Purpose of This Plan

This plan identifies who our stakeholders are, what their interests and influence are, and how we'll engage them effectively throughout the program lifecycle.

**Engagement Principles**:

- **Inclusive**: Diverse voices represented
- **Respectful**: Cultural safety and accessibility prioritized
- **Transparent**: Open communication about decisions
- **Reciprocal**: Mutual benefit and value exchange
- **Adaptive**: Responsive to stakeholder feedback

## Managing Difficult Stakeholders

### Stakeholder Resistance

**Scenario**: [Stakeholder group] opposes harm reduction approach

**Response**:

- **Listen**: Understand their concerns
- **Educate**: Share evidence base for harm reduction
- **Find common ground**: Shared goal of community wellbeing
- **Set boundaries**: Respectful dialogue required
- **Escalate if needed**: [To management, board]

---

### Competing Interests

**Scenario**: Partners have conflicting priorities

**Response**:

- **Clarify roles**: Review MOU/partnership agreement
- **Negotiate**: Find win-win solutions
- **Prioritize**: Participant wellbeing comes first
- **Document**: Record agreements in writing

---

### Over-Engagement

**Scenario**: Stakeholder demands excessive communication/input

**Response**:

- **Set boundaries**: Clarify appropriate engagement level
- **Batch communications**: Regular scheduled updates vs. ad-hoc
- **Delegate**: Appropriate team member manages relationship
- **Review**: If not improving, reassess partnership

## Feedback & Complaints Process

**Receiving Feedback**:

- Multiple channels: in-person, phone, email, anonymous box
- Documented in feedback register
- Acknowledged within 48 hours
- Responded to within 2 weeks

**Complaints Process**:

1. **Receive**: Via any channel, documented
2. **Acknowledge**: Within 24 hours
3. **Investigate**: Review facts, consult relevant parties
4. **Resolve**: Response provided, action taken if needed
5. **Learn**: Identify system improvements
6. **Follow-up**: Check resolution satisfactory

**Serious Complaints**: Escalated to management/board, NUAA complaints policy followed

## Cultural Safety in Engagement

**Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander Stakeholders**:

- **Consultation**: Seek guidance from Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander advisors
- **Protocols**: Follow cultural protocols for engagement
- **Representation**: Ensure Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander voices in advisory structures
- **Data sovereignty**: Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander people control their data

**LGBTIQ+ Stakeholders**:

- **Inclusive language**: Gender-neutral, LGBTIQ+ affirming
- **Safe spaces**: Anti-discrimination policies enforced
- **Representation**: LGBTIQ+ representation in governance/advisory

**CALD Stakeholders**:

- **Language access**: Interpreters, translated materials
- **Cultural competency**: Staff training
- **Community liaison**: Engage cultural community leaders

**Accessibility**:

- **Physical**: Wheelchair accessible venues
- **Sensory**: Accessible formats (large print, audio)
- **Cognitive**: Plain language, visual aids
- **Economic**: Transport support, no cost barriers
