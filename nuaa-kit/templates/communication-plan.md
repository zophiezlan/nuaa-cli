## 6. Crisis Communication

### Definition of Crisis

- Serious incident (death, injury, violence)
- Major privacy breach
- Significant negative media
- Organizational controversy affecting program

---

### Crisis Communication Protocol

1. **Immediate** (0-2 hours):
   - Assess situation
   - Alert senior management
   - Ensure participant/staff safety
   - Gather facts

2. **Short-term** (2-24 hours):
   - Convene crisis team
   - Prepare holding statement
   - Notify key stakeholders (funder, board)
   - Monitor media/social media

3. **Medium-term** (1-7 days):
   - Issue full statement if appropriate
   - Designated spokesperson responds to media
   - Update stakeholders regularly
   - Support affected parties

4. **Long-term** (ongoing):
   - Debrief and learn
   - Rebuild trust if needed
   - Update risk management
   - Review communication plan

**Crisis Spokesperson**: [Name, Role - typically CEO]
**Crisis Team**: [Names, Roles]

**Holding Statement Template**:

> "We are aware of [incident]. The safety and wellbeing of our participants and staff is our top priority. We are gathering information and will provide an update as soon as we can. For immediate enquiries, contact [name, number]."

### Language & Tone

**Principles**:
- **Stigma-free**: Avoid stigmatizing language
- **Person-first**: "People who use drugs" not "drug users"
- **Strengths-based**: Focus on capacity, not deficits
- **Plain language**: Accessible to all literacy levels
- **Respectful**: Dignity and respect for all people

**Avoid**:
- Junkie, addict, user (as noun), substance abuser
- Clean/dirty (for drug-free/using)
- Homeless (use "experiencing homelessness")

**Use**:
- Person who uses drugs, person who injects drugs
- Current/past drug use
- Harm reduction, peer-led, lived experience
- Empowerment, community, dignity, rights
